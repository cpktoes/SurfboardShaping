Shaper's notes

McKee quads — the 2009 front-fin line appears tuned for boards under 7′; use the pre-2009 line for longer boards. Front and back toe are read off the nose.

McKee ’12 — interpolated from B.C. McKee's June 2012 M4 Quattro / M5 Multisystem table: front and back positions by length, back-fin spread and toe (fin direction) by tail width at 12″ up, plus the M5 thruster rear-fin position. Pintail (+3/16″ back), narrow-tail cluster-forward, and small-board (≤5′10″) toe compensation are applied automatically from the tail-shape and length you set. Values are shown in inches with McKee's centimeters underneath. M4 Quattro and M5 Multisystem are trademarks of B.C. McKee; source: mckeesurf.com.

Rusty quad — back-off-rail and spread are general defaults that change with tail design; move the back fins closer to the rail on fish and wide-tail boards. (The back-off-tail figure carries the 1.125″ front-off-rail value forward from earlier in the original script — ported as written, worth a check against your own placements.)

Twin & longboard — twin front-off-tail comes from a sparse trendline and wants more data points. Longboard 2+1 front side-bites are usually around 14″ off the tail (range 13–17″).

Rail bands — the original table was built for a 2.5″ board and its top comment suggests scaling band marks by thickness ÷ 2.5. The code keeps band widths fixed and scales only the apex with thickness, so this page does the same. Adjust if you prefer the scaled approach.
