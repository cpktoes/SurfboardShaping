Fin Placement Calculator

Set your board's length and tail width and read off the box positions — off tail, off rail, toe and spread — for every common fin setup at once, side by side, so you can compare layouts before you drill a hole in your blank.

How to use it

Drag the length and tail-width sliders to your blank (or read them off calipers/story stick). Every card below updates instantly — thruster, quad, single, 2+1 and twin, all recalculated together, so you can compare layouts before you mark anything.

What the results mean

Off tail runs forward from the tail along the stringer. Off rail is measured in from the rail edge to the fin box, perpendicular to the stringer. Toe is the fin tip's inward offset toward the nose, read with a track square — not an angle. Spread is tip-to-tip distance between a pair's boxes.

Sanity-check numbers

Industry defaults worth cross-checking against: thruster side fins 11″–12″ off tail, 1⅛″–1¼″ off rail, front toe ¼″; center fin 3″–4″ off tail. Quad rear fins get ⅛″–⅜″ toe. Front cant runs roughly 5°–9°, rear 3°–5° (cant isn't computed here — no reliable formula, so treat it as a box-angle decision, not a slider).

Methodology & sources

McKee '09 quad — the 2009 front-fin line appears tuned for boards under 7′; use the pre-2009 line for longer boards. Front and back toe figures are read off the nose along the stringer, not at the fin itself.

McKee '12 — interpolated from B.C. McKee's June 2012 M4 Quattro / M5 Multisystem table: front and back off-tail by length, back-fin spread and toe by tail width at 12″ up, plus the M5 thruster rear-fin position. Pintail, narrow-tail cluster-forward, and small-board (≤5′10″) toe compensation apply automatically from the tail-shape and length you set. M4 Quattro and M5 Multisystem are trademarks of B.C. McKee; source: mckeesurf.com.

Rusty quad — back-off-rail and spread are general defaults that change with tail design; move the back fins closer to the rail on fish and wide-tail boards. Both quad lines here also carry a “center” off-tail figure from the original source table; a standard four-box quad has no center fin, so it's kept only as a reference number for boards that add a fifth, optional stabilizer box.

Twin & longboard — twin front-off-tail comes from a sparse trendline and wants more data points; sanity-check it against your own boards. Longboard 2+1 front side-bites are usually around 14″ off the tail (range 13–17″).

Toe & cant conventions — toe-in is a linear offset read with a track square (e.g. a Versa-Square), not a degree measurement; a common baseline is ¼″ for front tri/quad fins, ⅛″–⅜″ for rear quad fins, and roughly ⅜″ for twins. Cant (the fin's lean off vertical) isn't a linear or well-standardized-enough figure to compute here; 5–9° front and 3–5° rear is a commonly cited starting range — more cant loosens the board and reduces drive, less cant adds drive and projection. Sourced from Greenlight Surf Supply's fin-placement guide and FCS/Fiberglass Hawaii's fin box placement charts, alongside the McKee and Rusty tables above.

As with the rail-band calculator: treat every number here as a story-stick starting point, not gospel. Fine-tune by eye, by your own stance, and by how the board actually rides.
