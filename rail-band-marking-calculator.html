Rail Band Marking Calculator

Dial in your blank's thickness at the nose, center and tail, then read off the peak, tuck and band dimensions for ten common rail families — from a first foam job to your fiftieth.

How to use it

Drag the three sliders to your blank's thickness at the nose, center and tail (or read them straight off calipers). Switch units any time — every card below updates instantly for all ten rail families, at all three stations, so you can compare shapes before you cut foam.

What the results mean

Each card is one rail family's cross-section. The apex dot is where the deck bands begin, measured up from the bottom; the dashed peak sits mid-way up that same mark line, since that's where the finished, blended rail actually ends up widest, not at the raw corner. Tuck 1 and Tuck 2 are the two bottom-corner cuts; Band 1, Band 2 (and Band 3, on down rails) are the flat planes carrying the apex up to the deck. Dimensions live in the data box below each plot. Mark each point with a story stick, connect the dots, then blend with a planer or surform.

How to read a card

Every plot below follows this key. Each dot is colored to match its cut; the peak carries a short dash instead of a dot. Numbers live in the data box under each plot — connect the dots on your blank and follow the flow of the foil into the nose and tail.

Apex — dot; where Band 1 begins, measured up from the bottom
Rail peak — dash at the mark line's midpoint; the finished rail's true widest point
Tuck 1 — first cut: full tuck height on the edge, half the depth in along the bottom
Tuck 2 — second cut, continuing from Tuck 1's midpoint out to the full tuck depth
Band 1 — flat plane, apex to first deck point
Band 2 — carries Band 1 up to the deck
Band 3 — third plane, sharper down rails only

Methodology & sources

Rail-family shapes and band structure are compiled from published shaping references and long-running shaper community discussion — principally Greenlight Surf Supply's rail band guides and Swaylocks forum threads on marking and cutting rail bands. Dimensions are anchored to a 2½″ center-thickness reference board (a common convention in these sources) and scaled proportionally to your actual thickness at each station.

Every source is emphatic that these are starting points, not gospel: fine-tune by eye, and let the line follow the flow of your rocker and foil rather than chasing a number. Treat this tool as a story stick, not a rulebook.
